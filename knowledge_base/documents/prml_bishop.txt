Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning — Bishop (Curated Knowledge Base Excerpts)

Probability and Decision Theory
Pattern recognition formalizes inference under uncertainty. Bayes' theorem combines prior p(theta) with likelihood p(D|theta) to obtain posterior p(theta|D). Decision theory selects actions minimizing expected loss.

Linear Models for Regression
Linear regression with Gaussian noise yields closed-form maximum likelihood solutions. Regularized variants (Ridge) correspond to Gaussian priors on weights. Bayesian linear regression provides predictive distributions, not just point estimates.

Classification and Generative Models
Generative classifiers model class-conditional densities p(x|C) and priors p(C). Discriminative models directly model p(C|x). Logistic regression is a discriminative model with log-linear decision boundaries.

Neural Networks
Neural networks compose nonlinear transformations. Bishop discusses activation functions, universal approximation, and Bayesian treatments of network weights. Early stopping and weight decay act as regularizers.

Kernel Methods
Kernel functions implicitly map data to feature spaces where linear methods suffice. Gaussian processes provide non-parametric Bayesian regression with uncertainty estimates. RVM (Relevance Vector Machine) yields sparse kernel models.

Graphical Models
Directed (Bayesian networks) and undirected (Markov random fields) models encode conditional independence. Inference algorithms include belief propagation and variational methods.

Variational Inference and EM
The EM algorithm maximizes likelihood for models with latent variables by alternating expectation and maximization steps. Variational inference approximates intractable posteriors with simpler distributions.

Sampling Methods
MCMC (Metropolis-Hastings, Gibbs) draws samples from complex posteriors. Sampling enables Bayesian model averaging and uncertainty quantification.

Learning Theory
PAC learning and VC dimension characterize generalization bounds. Bishop connects capacity control, regularization, and Bayesian evidence to prevent overfitting.
